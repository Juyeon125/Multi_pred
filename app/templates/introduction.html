    EC number

    An abbreviation of enzyme code number. Method for systematic classification of enzymes. It consists of four pairs of numbers starting with EC. In 1961, the Enzyme Commission of the International Federation of Biochemistry established rules for the name and classification of enzymes, while issuing enzyme tables that systematically classify 707 enzymes, which were then known, and then assigned enzyme numbers to each enzyme. Some modifications have since been made to the rules to counter the development of biochemistry and the discovery of new enzymes. In the 1984 enzyme table, about 2,500 enzymes are classified as enzyme numbers.

    Utilize EC number

    Half a century ago, a large amount of new enzymes were discovered, and researchers in the pure bioscience, pathology and biochemical industries felt that rules for enzyme names were needed to take advantage of them, and enzyme naming conventions such as EC codes were developed to address them. The International Union of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology specifies an enzyme number that indicates the function of the enzyme. This could solve problems arising from ambiguous and unclear enzyme names in the field of enzyme utilization or publications dealing with enzyme research.

    Necessity of EC number prediction

    The enzyme number, which shows what kind of biochemical reaction enzymes intersect, is widely used in various fields. Currently, more than 10 computer methodologies have been developed to predict EC numbers, and DeepEC is showing outstanding performance in terms of predictive speed, accuracy, and predictable range. However, it is not easy for researchers who need enzyme numbers to use DeepEC. This is because DeepEC requires installation and use in a Linux environment, but not many researchers can handle Linux. That's why many researchers now use the amino acid sequence to sort out similar protein sequences in the Swiss-Prot (protein Sequence Database) and to find and guess the enzyme number among the information for each sequence. This method has the disadvantage of being cumbersome because it takes a long time to require many protein sequence enzyme numbers.
